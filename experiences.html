Here I´m sharing my most influencial travel experiences, which I still remember every day

My journey began bright and early on the morning of 9 September. I said good-bye to all my family and got into a flight from the Dresden Airport (DRS) to Frankfurt (FRA). There I already practised my Spanish skills by talking to Costa Rican people while waiting for my connecting flight to San José. Then it was time for boarding. As I set down, I noticed that the woman who was talking to me at the gate was exactly the same woman sitting next to me on my flight. What a coincidance! After a 13-hour-flight, I finally arrived in San José. The migration office was quite skeptical, because I didn´t have a hotel reservation, because I was staying with a host family, organized by a german agency. I'm glad I had a return ticket. I think otherwise they wouldn´t have let me into the Country. At the end, everything turned out just fine and I got a tourist visa for 180 days. I was picked up at the airport by a guy of the travel agency who drove me to the home of my host family. Here my mama-tica Alba already waited for me with a plate of Gallo Pinto (rice and beans), the tradtional Costa Rican food.
